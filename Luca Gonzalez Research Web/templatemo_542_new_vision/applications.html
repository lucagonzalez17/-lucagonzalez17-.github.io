Current use of Data Science in football

The big change in football has occurred thanks to the rapid development of tracking technologies that follow all of the players and the ball and are used in combination with other technologies such as accelerometers, gyroscopes ,hear rate monitors or other devices for measuring physiological parameters.Inside of this tracking devices we can differentiate between Optical-based camera systems,LPS(Local Positioning Systems) and GPS(Satellite systems).About eight million data are collected each match thanks to all of these technologies.(Rodriguez,2020As said before ,the data by its own is nothing so football clubs need to invest in professionals capable of analysing all kinds of data in order to optimize their performance.At this moment is when the figure of the data scientist arises. The data is obtained though supplier companies in the sector such as: Opta,InStat,WyScout,MediaCoach,NacSport,Brand24 and AzSportTech. For example WyScout is a company that centralizes all the video information of all the matches and players of all competitions worldwide.Related article.

Each team has its own organizational dynamics regarding the analysis but according to the experts on the field the work can be explained as follows.

Data mining in football:

• Predictive game models

• Models of propensity to injury or fatigue

• Individual vs global team analysis

• Simulations of plays and confrontations

• Valuation of market players

Objectives of a Data Analyst

Is important to clarify which is mainly what football clubs ask of a data analyst.Data scientists (a) need to know concepts of the own team and the rival team examining what metrics show them about different aspects of the game.After this,the professionals must (b) analyze the percentages of effectiveness and weaknesses in different phases of the game as explained before.Now analyst need to (c) understand what content is better to optimize when you are going to compete and be able to transmit it to the coach.The confidence of the coach in his data team varies from one team to another but it is very difficult if not impossible to find one that ignores Big Data because they can become obsolete.
